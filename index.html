How it works

Four-week simulation

A market game with a real learning arc.

Students manage a pretend portfolio, watch a leaderboard shift, and use each weekly check-in to study emotion, risk, strategy, and evidence—not secret stock tips.

Week 01

Surviving red days

Students learn why falling prices can feel scary, then compare market panic to a sale at a store.

  • Market emotion
  • Loss aversion
  • Process over picks
Week 02

The castle strategy

Students explore defensive investing by asking what makes a company resilient when conditions change.

  • Risk tolerance
  • Durable advantages
  • Strategy discipline
Week 03

The home stretch

Students reflect on decision-making as the leaderboard tightens and short-term noise gets louder.

  • Staying the course
  • News versus signal
  • Decision journals
Week 04

Graduation day

The simulation closes with a final review: what worked, what failed, and what students would test next.

  • Final leaderboard
  • Portfolio reflection
  • Real-world transfer

Big connections

Capital markets have some unexpected neighbors.

Zoom out and market ideas start to rhyme with physics and math. Prices can balance like forces, ripple like waves, and organize like particles in a crowd.
