The 5-day curriculum

Five focused lessons.
One market simulation.

The week starts with ownership and ends with a one-month paper trading simulation using real market data and fake money.

Day 01

Stop being just a customer

Students learn that a stock is a piece of a company and compare public companies with private companies.

  • Shares and stocks
  • Public vs. private
  • Brand audit challenge
Day 02

The rollercoaster

Students use sneaker drops, news, and supply and demand to explain why prices change every day.

  • Supply and demand
  • Good news and bad news
  • News detective challenge
Day 03

ETFs and diversification

Students learn why one company can be risky and how ETFs spread ownership across many companies.

  • Never one basket
  • ETF variety pack
  • Dream ETF challenge
Day 04

Dividends and time

Students learn how some companies pay cash rewards to shareholders and compare growth with income.

  • Dividend rewards
  • Growth vs. income
  • Dividend hunt
Day 05

The simulation begins

Students enter a one-month market simulation, make a first purchase, and meet weekly for leaderboard check-ins.

  • Fake money
  • Real market data
  • First stock or ETF

Core concepts

The ideas students
learn to use.

Each concept is taught through a simple image: puzzle pieces, sneaker drops, baskets, playlists, money trees, and a market arena.

01

Stocks are ownership

A public company can be sliced into shares. Buying one means owning a tiny percentage.

puzzle pieces
02

Prices move for a reason

High demand and low supply push prices up. Bad news and panic can push prices down.

sneaker drop
03

Diversification protects you

Spreading money across companies means one bad result does not wipe out everything.

egg basket
04

ETFs are bundles

One ETF share can hold tiny pieces of hundreds of companies, like a playlist of stocks.

variety pack
05

Dividends pay owners

Some companies share profits with shareholders as cash rewards every few months.

rent check
